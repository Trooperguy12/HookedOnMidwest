Bass: Early Morning Bite

Fish for largemouth bass at dawn using topwater lures like frogs to catch them feeding near lily pads.

Bass: Summer Heat Strategy

In hot Midwest summers, target bass in deeper waters with crankbaits to find them hiding near drop-offs.

Catfish: Night Fishing

Catfish are more active at night; use stink bait or chicken liver on a bottom rig in slow-moving rivers.

Catfish: Muddy Water Tactics

After rain, catfish move to shallow, muddy waters‚Äîtry using a slip sinker rig with cut bait for best results.

Panfish: Spring Spawn

Target bluegill and crappie near shorelines during spring spawn with small jigs or live worms under a bobber.

Panfish: Ice Fishing

In winter, panfish like perch school in deep basins‚Äîuse tiny jigs tipped with wax worms through the ice.

Trout: Stream Fishing

In Midwest streams, trout hide behind rocks‚Äîcast small spinners upstream and let them drift naturally.

Trout: Cold Weather Tips

In colder months, trout slow down; use live bait like minnows or powerbait on a light line for better bites.
